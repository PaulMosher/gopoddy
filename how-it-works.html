The Poddy — How It Works

Simpler than a bag.
Cleaner than you'd expect.

Two minutes. No plastic. No smell. Here's exactly what using and cleaning the Poddy looks like — step by step, walk to toilet and back again.

Part One

On the Walk

Three steps. No bag to remember. One device handles the whole walk.

1
Clip & Go

Grab the Poddy. That's it.

The Poddy clips to your belt, leash, or bag. No bag to remember, no running out, no counting. One device handles the whole walk — two poops, three poops, whatever the morning brings.

2
Scoop

Open, scoop, seal.

The built-in claw retrieves the waste — no hand contact, no thin plastic between you and the problem. The flared opening makes transfer clean. Seal the lid and you're done. The odor-proof seal locks everything inside until you get home.

3
Keep Walking

No bag to carry. No trash can to find.

The sealed Poddy clips back to your belt. It doesn't swing, smell, or remind you it's there. Go further. Take the trail you usually skip because of the "what if." The Poddy goes wherever you and your dog go.

Why It Works

The odor-proof seal is the whole game.

Most people assume a container with waste in it will smell. It doesn't — because the Poddy is sealed. You can walk past people on the trail, ride home in your car, hang it on the stroller — and no one knows it's there. That's the point of the design.

Part Two

Getting Home — The Empty

Three steps. Straight to the toilet. Under a minute.

4
Setup

Turn on the bathroom light and fan.

Flip on the fan timer before you start. Open the lid and seat. Two seconds of prep that make everything easier.

5
Claw First

Rest the claw on the toilet rim.

Open the Poddy and use the built-in rim rest to set the claw section on the edge of the toilet bowl. A quick rinse in the clean toilet water takes care of any residue on the claw — no sink needed.

6
The Empty

Tilt toward the bowl. Don't aim for the water.

Grab a couple squares of toilet paper in one hand. Slowly tilt the Poddy over the toilet. Aim for the porcelain, not the water — this avoids splash. You can lay a little toilet paper in the bowl first if you want an extra buffer.

While the container is still tilted, use your toilet paper to wipe the rim — working from the underside upward. Turn the container upright, toss the tissue, flush.

The Technique

Come up from the underside.

This is the one thing that keeps the outside of the Poddy clean — and it's not complicated. When you're emptying and wiping, everything moves from the underside of the container upward. You're wiping the rim as you tilt back to upright. The motion keeps any residue on the inside, where it belongs.

One motion. Every time. The outside stays clean.

Part Three

The Rinse — Only When You Need It

Three steps. After a messier walk, here's the full rinse process — about three minutes of actual effort.

7
Quick Check

If the inside looks decent — just seal it.

The odor-proof seal means you don't need to clean after every walk. Seal the lid and the Poddy is ready for the next outing. This is the most common outcome after a firm-stool walk.

8
Sink Rinse

Let the water do the work.

With the claw still resting on the toilet rim, run water from the sink faucet down the walls of the container while you slowly rotate it. Cold water is fine — and better for the planet. The water rinses the walls as it flows. Leave enough water in the container that when you seal the lid, the claw is submerged too — that way it soaks clean alongside everything else. Never dump the rinse water in the sink.

9
Passive Soak

Seal it. Walk away. Flush later.

Sealing the lid is what traps the smell inside. Set the Poddy behind the toilet and leave it. If you want to conserve water, add the contents to the toilet during your next bathroom visit — everything will have loosened and settled by then. Otherwise, empty and flush whenever it's convenient. The Poddy cleaned itself while you went about your day.

A note on stool quality

Firm waste (Purina Fecal Score 2–3) leaves little to no residue and rarely requires anything more than a seal and you're done. If your dog consistently has loose stools, the Poddy still works — but consider your dog's diet. Stool quality directly affects the cleanup experience, and a simple food adjustment often makes a significant difference.
