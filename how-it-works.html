The Poddy — How It Works

Simpler than a bag.
Cleaner than you'd expect.

Two minutes. No plastic. No smell. Here's exactly what using and cleaning the Poddy looks like — step by step, walk to toilet and back again.

Part One

On the Walk

1
Clip & Go

Grab the Poddy. That's it.

The Poddy clips to your belt, leash, or bag. No bag to remember, no running out, no counting. One device handles the whole walk — two poops, three poops, whatever the morning brings.

2
Scoop

Open, scoop, seal.

The built-in claw retrieves the waste — no hand contact, no thin plastic between you and the problem. The flared opening makes transfer clean. Seal the lid and you're done. The odor-proof seal locks everything inside until you get home.

3
Keep Walking

No bag to carry. No trash can to find.

The sealed Poddy clips back to your belt. It doesn't swing, smell, or remind you it's there. Go further. Take the trail you usually skip. The Poddy goes wherever you and your dog go.

The odor-proof seal is the whole game.

Most people assume a container with waste in it will smell. It doesn't — because the Poddy is sealed. You can walk past people on the trail, ride home in your car, hang it on the stroller — and no one knows it's there.

Part Two

Getting Home — The Empty

4
Setup

Turn on the bathroom light and fan.

Flip on the fan timer before you start. Open the lid and seat. Two seconds of prep that make everything easier.

5
Claw First

Rest the claw on the toilet rim.

Open the Poddy and use the built-in rim rest to set the claw section on the edge of the toilet bowl. A quick rinse in the clean toilet water takes care of any residue on the claw — no sink needed.

6
The Empty

Tilt toward the bowl. Don't aim for the water.

Grab a couple squares of toilet paper in one hand. Slowly tilt the Poddy over the toilet. Aim for the porcelain, not the water — this avoids splash. You can lay a little toilet paper in the bowl first if you want an extra buffer.

While the container is still tilted, use your toilet paper to wipe the rim — working from the underside upward. Turn the container upright, toss the tissue, flush.

The Technique

Come Up From the Underside.

This is the one thing that keeps the outside of the Poddy clean. When you're emptying and wiping, everything moves from the underside of the container upward. You wipe the rim as you tilt back to upright. The motion keeps any residue on the inside, where it belongs.

"It's all about coming up from the underside of the container after dumping. This is how you keep the germs limited to the inside of the Poddy."

One motion. Every time. The outside stays clean.

Part Three

The Rinse — When You Need It

After a firm-stool walk, there's often nothing to clean. Seal it and it's ready for tomorrow. After a messier walk, here's the full rinse process.

7
Quick Check

If the inside looks decent — just seal it.

The odor-proof seal means you don't need to clean after every walk. Seal the lid and the Poddy is ready for the next outing. This is the most common outcome after a firm-stool walk.

8
Sink Rinse

Let the water do the work.

If the inside is messy, tilt it under the sink and slowly let warm water rinse down the sides of the container. Don't fill it — turn the water off before it gets more than 80% full. Seal the lid. Set it behind the toilet and leave it.

9
Passive Soak

Let it sit. The debris settles itself.

Leave the Poddy behind the toilet until your next bathroom visit. By then, everything has loosened and settled. Tilt it over the toilet slowly — have tissue ready — wipe the rim coming upward, flush, done.

10
Claw Soak

If the claw needs it — drop it in the water.

If the claw is messy from the walk, open the Poddy, drop the claw into the soaking water, and reseal. Same passive soak. By the time you're next in the bathroom, it's clean.

A note on stool quality

Firm waste (Purina Fecal Score 2–3) leaves little to no residue and rarely requires anything more than a seal. If your dog consistently has loose stools, consider their diet. Stool quality directly affects the cleanup experience.

Common Questions

Things People Ask Before Their First Walk

Do I have to clean the Poddy after every walk?

No. The odor-proof seal means you can close it after use and leave it sealed until your next walk. When stool is firm, there is often little to no residue at all.

How do I keep the outside clean?

Technique. When emptying, tilt toward the toilet and wipe the rim from the underside upward as you return the container to upright. This keeps any residue inside. It becomes second nature after a few walks.

What if my dog's stool is loose?

The Poddy still works — it just requires a rinse rather than a quick seal. If loose stool is consistent, consider your dog's diet. Firmer stool makes the experience noticeably easier.

Can I flush dog waste down the toilet?

Yes — the EPA recommends it as the most environmentally sound residential disposal method. Dog waste contains similar pathogens to human waste and municipal wastewater treatment is designed to handle it. Check local regulations if you're on a septic system.

How long does it actually take?

A quick empty: under two minutes. A full rinse: three to five minutes total, most of which is the Poddy sitting behind the toilet soaking on its own.

Does it smell in the house?

No. The odor-proof seal contains everything. That's also why you can carry it clipped to your belt past other people on a trail — no one knows it's there.
