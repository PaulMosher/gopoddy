The Problem Isn’t Just the Bag

The most sustainable bag
is the one you never use.

U.S. dog owners use approximately 27 billion plastic bags every year. Bags are only the most visible part of the problem — dog waste also pollutes waterways, threatens public health, and represents over a billion dollars in renewable energy and fertilizer value sent straight to landfills.

The Poddy is the only product designed to address all three. It eliminates the bag, routes waste to the wastewater system the EPA recommends, and turns a daily nuisance into something better for your walk, your community, and the planet.

A note on the numbers: the U.S. has approximately 90 million dogs total. About 72 million live in suburban and urban areas where a collection system makes sense — the addressable market for the Poddy. Bag totals on this page reflect total U.S. usage; resource-value and adoption math throughout the site is built on the addressable 72 million.
